## Buying Guide for Budget Monitors

When searching for the best budget monitor, consider the following factors:

### 1. Screen Size and Resolution
Choose a monitor size that fits your workspace and meets your productivity needs. A 24-inch screen with a Full HD (1920 x 1080) resolution is often a sweet spot for most users.

### 2. Panel Type
- **IPS Panels**: Offer better color accuracy and viewing angles, ideal for creative work.
- **TN Panels**: Generally cheaper but have poorer color reproduction and viewing angles, suitable for basic tasks.
- **VA Panels**: Balanced performance for color and contrast but can be pricier.

### 3. Connectivity Options
Ensure the monitor has the necessary ports (HDMI, DisplayPort, VGA) to connect to your devices. Additional USB ports can also be a plus.

### 4. Ergonomics
Look for monitors with adjustable stands (height, tilt, swivel) to ensure comfort during long working hours.

### 5. Eye Care Features
Consider monitors with flicker-free technology and low blue light modes if you spend extended periods in front of the screen.

### 6. Price
Set a budget and look for monitors that provide the best value within that range. Always compare features to ensure you're getting the best deal.
